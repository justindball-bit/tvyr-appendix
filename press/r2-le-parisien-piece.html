Teacher Suspended After Showing RawHistory.wtf Footage To Senior Students

A history teacher at one of France's most prestigious lycées showed Release Two to her terminale class. She has not apologized. She is not going to.
By Mathieu Renard, Staff Correspondent

The incident occurred on a Tuesday morning in a history classroom at the Lycée Henri-IV in the 5th arrondissement, one of the most prestigious secondary schools in France. The teacher, identified as Sylvie Marchand, 41, a history and geography instructor with fourteen years at the institution, showed approximately twenty-three students in her terminale class what she described in subsequent communications as "a primary historical document of exceptional significance." The document in question was the Release Two footage from RawHistory.wtf. The footage of Grigori Rasputin. She showed it without warning, without parental consent, and without, according to the school administration's statement released Friday, any prior discussion with department colleagues or supervisory staff. She has been suspended pending a full disciplinary review. She has not apologized.

What Happened In The Classroom

The accounts of students present are consistent in their broad outline and varied in their details in the way that witness accounts always are when something unexpected occurs in a contained space. The footage ran for its full six minutes and forty seconds. Marchand stood at the front of the room and watched her students watching it. Several students reported that she did not watch the screen. She watched them. When the footage ended she asked the class a single question. "What did you feel?"

The responses, according to three students who agreed to speak to Le Parisien on condition of anonymity, ranged from silence to visible distress to, in at least two cases, something the students described with difficulty as the opposite of distress. One student — a seventeen year old girl who asked that nothing further be said about her — reportedly said that she felt she had seen something true. Marchand, by multiple accounts, nodded at this as though it confirmed something. The class ended. Students went to their next lesson. Two went directly to the school counselor. One went home. One, according to a source familiar with the situation, went to find Marchand after school to continue the conversation. By Wednesday morning the administration had received eleven parent complaints.

Who Is Sylvie Marchand

Colleagues describe her as a committed, rigorous teacher with a particular interest in Russian history and the late Romanov period. She has published two papers in academic journals on the role of mysticism in pre-Revolutionary Russian society. She is described as private, professional, and until this week, entirely unremarkable in terms of institutional behavior.

What her colleagues did not know — what the administration did not know until Thursday morning when a parent forwarded a screenshot — is that Marchand has been active on social media under a pseudonym since Release Two of RawHistory.wtf dropped. Her account, which we are not naming, has 34,000 followers. It is one of the accounts associated with the growing online movement around the footage. It has posted photographs of Rasputin's tomb site. It has posted what can only be described as devotional content. In one post, published eleven days ago, she wrote: He was given to us and we destroyed him and now we are being shown what we threw away. This is not history. This is a reckoning. In another, published four days before she showed the footage to her class: The young ones need to see it. They are not yet closed. They can still receive it. The administration became aware of these posts on Thursday. The suspension was issued Friday morning.

The Statement

Marchand has not spoken to the press directly. Through a representative — not a lawyer, a friend, identity unknown — she released a written statement on Friday evening.

I showed my students a document that I believe is among the most significant historical and spiritual materials to emerge in my lifetime. I showed it to them because I am a history teacher and it is history, and because I am a person who believes that the young have a right to encounter the true and the real before the world teaches them to be afraid of both. I have read the parent complaints. I understand the concern. I do not share it. What my students saw in that classroom is what humanity has always been, in its fullness, without the editorial interventions of institutions that have always preferred a manageable version of the truth to the truth itself. Grigori Rasputin was a man through whom something moved that the modern world has no vocabulary for. The footage shows this. My students saw this. Some of them felt it. I watched their faces and I saw the moment it arrived in them and I will not apologize for giving them that. I will also say this, because I think the record should contain it: the student who stayed after class. The one who came to find me. She understood. She asked me where the tomb was. I told her. What she does with that information is her own. I have been a teacher for fourteen years. I have never taught anything more true than what I taught on Tuesday morning. I am not sorry.

The Students

The administration has offered counseling to all students present. Uptake has been, according to a source, mixed. Several parents have described their children as distressed, unable to sleep, or unwilling to discuss what they saw. One parent, who asked not to be named, described her sixteen year old son as having watched the footage three more times at home on his own phone after the classroom screening. When she asked him why he said he was trying to understand something. He has not elaborated further.

Another parent described her daughter as having become, in the days since the screening, quieter than usual. Not distressed. Quieter. "Like she's thinking about something she won't tell me," the parent said. "She's always told me everything. Since Tuesday she's somewhere I can't reach." The student who stayed after class to speak to Marchand has not been identified. The school is aware of the conversation but has declined to comment on it specifically. Her parents have not come forward.

The Broader Context

This is not the first time a teacher has shown RawHistory.wtf footage in a classroom. It is the first documented case involving Release Two. It is also the first documented case where the teacher's motivation appears to be not pedagogical but devotional. The distinction matters. Marchand did not show her students the footage of Rasputin because it illuminates the late Romanov period or complicates the historical record around a controversial figure. She showed it because she believes Rasputin was a vessel for something divine and she wanted her students to feel what she feels when she watches it. That is not a history lesson. It is something the French educational system does not currently have a category for and is, according to sources within the Ministry of Education, actively trying to develop one.

The disciplinary hearing is scheduled for the 14th. Sylvie Marchand is, according to the friend who delivered her statement, calm. "She says she knew this would happen," the friend told Le Parisien. "She says it doesn't matter. She says what she gave those students on Tuesday morning cannot be taken back." She is right about that last part.

Le Parisien requested comment from RawHistory.wtf. No response was received. The site's public statement, released following Release Two, remains pinned to the landing page. Membership, the site notes, is open. The student who stayed after class could not be reached for comment.
